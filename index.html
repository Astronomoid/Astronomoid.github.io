Introduction

My name is Rishabh Singh Teja. I am currently a Senior Research Fellow (SRF) at the Indian Institute of Astrophysics (IIA) situated at Bangalore , Karnataka, India. I have been doing my PhD here. I work on the observational and theoretical aspects of Core-Collapse Supernovae (CCSNe), which are the spectacular deaths of stars with initial masses greater than 8 Suns combined.

Till now I have worked on the data from the following telescopes:
  • Himalayan Chandra Telescope (2-m)
  • Devasthal Optical Telescope (3.6-m)
  • UVOT onboard Swift Observatory (0.3-m)
  • Growth India Telescope (0.7-m)
  • KANATA Telescope, Hiroshima, Japan (1-5 m)
  • AstroSat (UVIT)
I am familier with the following tools, computer languages, codes, etc., some of which I use very often.
  • Languages: C, C++, and Python
  • Tools/Softwares: MESA Star, IRAF, SYNAPPS, TARDIS, Linux, and MS-Office
  • Others: Latex, HTML, CSS, and Javascript
